# ☕ Coffee Shop Sales Analysis & Dashboard (6-Month Study)

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes sales data from a coffee shop business over a **6-month period across 3 store locations**.  

The goal was to uncover insights into sales performance, customer purchasing behavior, and product trends to support better business decision-making.

---

## 🎯 Business Objectives
- Evaluate store performance across locations  
- Identify top-performing products and categories  
- Analyze customer purchasing patterns by time and day  
- Discover opportunities to increase revenue and optimize operations  

---

## ⚙️ Tools & Technologies
- SQL (Databricks) – Data cleaning and transformation  
- Microsoft Excel – Data analysis, pivot tables, and dashboard creation  
- XMind – Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) planning  
- PowerPoint (PPTX) – Presentation of insights  

---

## 🧹 Data Preparation & Feature Engineering
- Standardized column names and formats to eliminate inconsistencies  
- Cleaned and structured a large transactional dataset  
- Created new features:
  - Day name
  - Month name 
  - Day of month
  - Day classificatin
  - Time classification
  - Spend category
  - Revenue
- Processed data using SQL in Databricks and exported to Excel  

---

## 📈 Analysis & Dashboard
Analysis was conducted in Excel using:
- Pivot Tables  
- Charts and visualizations  
- Interactive dashboard  

The dashboard enables quick comparison of store performance and sales trends.

---

## 🔍 Key Metrics Analyzed
- Sales by Time of Day  
- Revenue by Day of Week  
- Sales Trends Over Time  
- Top Product Categories  
- Product Performance  
- Store Performance  
- Spend Category Distribution  
- Quantity Sold by Category  

---

## 💡 Key Insights
- Sales are relatively evenly distributed across all three stores
- Sales declined between the 27th and 31st
- Late morning is the peak sales period , highest customer activity and revenue occur during this time
- Certain product categories drive most of the revenue such as tea and coffee   
- Customer behavior varies by day of the week with monday having the most sales

---

## 📊 Business Recommendations
- Maintain consistency across stores by standardizing best practices in service, product quality, and operations  
- Use high and low peak hours for promotions and upselling  
- Focus on high-performing products  
- Increase staff during peak late morning hours   
- Use targeted marketing strategies  
  - Introduce promotions during the 27th–31st dip  
  - Apply loyalty programs and limited-time offers
